Our last trip to Malaysia ironically was at the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, in February 2020. At that time news of the mysterious new virus emanated from Wuhan, China, and it seemed similar to the SARS epidemic from a few years back.

In fact, stories had begun to circulate even before we left, and we were considering whether we should postpone or cancel the trip. In the end, we were glad we could make it, as it was the last time I saw both my parents alive.

My father had an inkling that we would not see each other again, and he made us say formal farewells to each other, as if it would be our final days together.

In hindsight, he was right. A few weeks after we returned, countries began to enact border closures, the WHO declared the COVID-19 pandemic, and the world plunged into lockdown.

In the midst of all this, both my parents died, not from COVID-19, but from other causes (my father died after a series of strokes, and my mother had been of ill heath for a number of years).

Eventually, after vaccines were developed, lockdowns were lifted, and the world gradually reopened for international travel, and we contemplated travelling back to Malaysia.

But international travcel had become much more expensive post COVID-19 lockdown. Airlines were restarting their operations and it seemed it would take a while before things returned back to "normal", and perhaps this was the new normal.

So we were glad when AirAsia announced a 50% sale for all tickets, so we were able to book Premium Flatbed seats to Malaysia and back. I had also given up my Qantas Club membership during the lockdown, so I had no special loyalty towards Qantas, especially after all the negative media reports of cancelled flights and other disruptions.

We decided to spend 3 days in Johor Bahru, and a week in Kuala Lumpur. I was excited, as I was not sure how Malaysia has changed since the pandemic, but we were really looking forward to the trip, as it involved catching up with family and friends.

In the end, we were really glad we made the trip. I really missed all the food, although everything is now more expensive. And surprisingly, I bought a lot of clothes and shoes - so much that I had to buy a new suitcase to fit everything in!
